Generational Toxin
A poem by Joey Liu

A new wave quickly washes ashore

Along with the tide brings on a beached whale

Ready to explode, ready to derail

Into a toxin, humans have ingested before.

This toxin, it moves around and around,
It lurks through the air but poisons the ground.

The infected experience symptoms indeed.
-By shoving our elders to the curb,
-Disrespecting our beliefs,
-Assaulting our people and making them bleed.

Yet no one responds as it lurks through the air.
This toxin, this toxin, no one seems to care.

“Go back to your country” The infected they say,
Resembling the predator attacking its prey.

Subway stations, salons, sidewalks and streets,
Where are we safe from the infected elites?

Soon this toxin makes it way to the top,
Now the head is infected, it coughs and it coughs,
“Kung flu” and “China Virus” echo through the chamber of thought.
Now millions are infected, it cannot be stopped.

When warned of this toxin, too many are dumbfound.
Our struggles, our demands, they all get shot down.
"Don't worry" they say, "You've no right to that frown;"
"Model minorities are meant to be crowns!"

A byproduct of times where the light shined so bright.

The light is what leads the whales to the beach.

So many whales have surrendered the sea,

releasing a toxin that cannot be seen.

This toxin, humans have ingested before,
Left from all the dead carcasses washed up on shore.
